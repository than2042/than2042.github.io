Boris Johnson

Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs

Alexander Boris de Pfeffel Johnson born 19 June 1964, known as Boris Johnson, is a British politician, popular historian and journalist. He has been Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs since 2016.

Early Life

Childhood 1964-1967

Johnson was born on 19 June 1964 at a hospital on the Upper East Side of Manhattan in New York City, to British parents. Johnson has described himself as a "one-man melting pot" – with a combination of Muslims, Jews, and Christians as great-grandparents. Johnson and his siblings being sent to Ashdown House preparatory boarding school in East Sussex in 1975. There he developed a love of rugby and excelled at Ancient Greek and Latin.

Eton and Oxford 1977-1987

Johnson was awarded a King's Scholarship to study at Eton College, the elite independent boarding school in Eton. Although school reports complained about his idleness, complacency, and lateness, he established himself as a popular and well-known figure within the school.
Johnson won a scholarship to read Literae Humaniores, a four-year course based in the study of Classics, at Balliol College, Oxford. Johnson became a popular and well-known public figure at Oxford.

Early Career

He then, in late 1987 begun work as a graduate trainee at The Time, shadowing on of its journalists by through family connection. He was sacked because he wrote an article on the archeaological discovery of Edward II's palace for the newspaper but he falsely claimed come form the historican Colin Lucas, his godfather.

He secured employment on the Leader writing desk of The Daily Telegraph, having known its editor, Max Hasting. However, Hasting turned down him request t become a war reporter, instead promoting him as assistant editor and chief polotical columnist. Boris Johnson's column received prised for ideologically electric and uniquely written, and earned him a Commentator of the Year Award of the "What the Paper Say Awards". He was also accused of bigotry; in one of his column he use the words Piccannies and Watermelon Smiles referring to Africans and championed European colonialism in Uganda. He also referred to gay men as tank-topped bumboys.

MP for Henley 1999-2008

Johnson decided to stand as the Conservative candidate for Henley, a Conservative safe seat in Oxfordshire.The local Conservative branch were split over Johnson's candidacy; some found him amusing and charming, while others were critical of his flippant attitude to serious matters and his lack of knowledge about the local area, although he was nevertheless selected.

Mayor of London 2008-2016

March 2007, Johnson suggested that he stand for the position of Mayor of London. His campaign capitalised on his popularity, even among those who opposed his policies, with opponents complaining that a common attitude among voters was that "I'm voting for Boris because he is a laugh".
Johnson made no major changes to the mayoral system as developed by Livingstone. He introduced a public bicycle scheme which had been mooted by Livingstone's administration; colloquially known as "Boris Bike".

Foreign Secretary: 2016–Present

After Theresa May became leader of the Conservative and Prime Minister, Johnson was appointed as Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs on 13 July 2016. In August 2016, May called on "feuding ministers" Johnson and Liam Fox to 'stop playing games' after Fox wrote to Johnson saying that British trade couldn't flourish whilst future policy responsibility remained in Johnson's department.
